Non-biodegradable materials and their effects

Non-biodegradable materials are ones that cannot be broken down by the environment-air, sunlight, water or ground soil substances. These products have a longer-lasting effect on the environment.examples:plasticsbags.

1. The following are harmful effects of non-biodegradable waste:

I. Groundwater Contamination

Long-term exposure to the environment can cause petroleum-based plastics to emit toxic pollutants. Bisphenol A is a chemical used in the production of water bottles and other hard plastics. Low doses of this pollutant have been detected in groundwater sources.

II. Out-gassing

VI. Harm wildlife

Polystyrene can also harm marine wildlife. When foam enters the ocean, it has a tendency to break down into smaller, non-biodegradable pieces. Small fish and birds accidentally ingest these pieces, endangering their overall well being.
